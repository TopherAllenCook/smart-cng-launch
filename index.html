The strategy

We sell uptime,
not hardware.

Smart CNG isn't a feature on the FMM. It's how American CNG becomes the company that keeps fleets running, and owns the aftermarket that follows.

01
Who we're selling to

The fleet buyer cares about three things, in this order.

01
Does it finish the route?
Every truck, every day. A parked truck is a missed route and a penalty call.
02
Can I find a tech?
65.5% of diesel shops are understaffed. Fewer people to fix more trucks.
03
Does the 15-year math work?
They keep trucks 10-15 years. Lifecycle cost, not sticker price.

What they don't buy on: "green." They buy the economics green enables. Lead with the dollar. The carbon-negative story is the closing gift.

Sources: ATRI 2025; Ryder fleet TCO.
02
The buying committee

A committee of skeptics. Each one holds a veto.

Fleet Director
TCO, uptime data, parts standardization.
VP Operations
"Will it finish the day?" Route coverage.
Shop Manager
Ease of service, technician burden.
Procurement
Capital cost, incentive capture.
Sustainability
CI scores, ESG, RNG eligibility.

We win the room by handing each one their veto-killer, and uptime is the one they all share.

03
Why now
$5.52
on-highway diesel per gallon, up $2 in a year. California is already past $7.
$2.96
CNG per gallon-equivalent, flat for a decade. Domestic. Price-stable.

A $2.50 spread, and widening. Diesel is up two dollars in twelve months. CNG hasn't moved in ten years. And electric still can't run the route.

Sources: EIA on-highway diesel (May 2026); AFDC CNG; Fox10TV/WALA 2024.
03
The gap today

A fuel-system failure isn't a breakdown. It's a cascade.

Faults show up as a dead truck mid-route, not a warning
CNG diagnosis needs a specialist who isn't there at 6 AM
One failure = blocked route, second truck, tow, transfer. 4-6 hours gone.

A truck out of service costs $1,000-$2,000 a day. The average fleet eats 8.7 unplanned days a year.

Sources: Highway & Heavy Parts; Ryder.
04
The product, and I know it cold

Smart CNG: intelligence inside the FMM.

01
Monitor
Continuous reads on pressure, temperature, flow, solenoid state, fill cycles.
02
Detect
Flags a degrading regulator or slow leak in real time, on the road.
03
Alert
Pushes the diagnosis to operators and shop, before the next PM interval.
04
Resolve
Names the exact part SKU and triggers fulfillment. Closed loop.

Built into the Fuel Management Module, not a bolt-on. Market-ready Q4 2026.

05
In the cab

The brains live in the FMM. The face lives on the dash.

Smart CNG does the thinking: monitor, predict, name the part
The Smart Gauge is where the driver sees it: range, pressure, the warning
One system. Intelligence underneath, clarity on the dash.
06
Why we win

Everyone else is blind to the tank.

Telematics
Samsara, Geotab. Read the engine ECU. Blind to the fuel system.
CNG gauges
Blue iQ, GreenLync. Show fuel level. React after a tech plugs in.
Only us
Smart CNG
Predicts the fault and names the part before the truck stops.

The aligned-incentive moat: a service company profits when trucks break down. We profit when they keep running. Only the FMM maker can build prediction in, and the advantage compounds with every system on the road.

The commercial play

It sells iron today.
It becomes recurring tomorrow.

Now: wins more FMM deals + captures parts that leak to aftermarket
Day one: a small per-truck fee, so it's priced, not free
Later: a platform subscription on a moat of fleet-wide fault data

Year-one metric isn't Smart CNG revenue. It's FMM deals won where Smart CNG was the deciding factor. We test the pricing in the beta.
